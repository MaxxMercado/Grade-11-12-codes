Obtaining Information



What is Information?

It is the data collected provided or learned by a particular circumstance that is received and processed primarily to inform oneself. Good information proves more valuable as sources of knowledge to be utilized by people.


Importance of Information

Allowing the people to have access to good information, the knowledge received may help establish better connections with the community, or even influence the public consciousness. Good information is essential for effective operation and decision making at all levels in business. This does not only mean the significance of information, but also why it is crucial for it to be a good one.


How to obtain Information

  • Questionnaires, surveys and checklists A list or sets of questions given to the sources or people of information to be answered.

  • Personal interviews
    One-on-one discussions with the person to interview and the interviewee, usually used when you want to fully understand a person’s opinions or point of view or to get additional information from the set of questions given.

  • Documentation review
    Used to gather information without interrupting the program itself by examining program monitoring reports and statistics, learner progress reports, annual reports, memos, minutes of the meetings, performance appraisals, board evaluations, written policies and procedures, memos, minutes, etc.

  • Observation
    The five senses are used as certain information is gathered carefully from primary sources observed in everyday lives.

  • Focus group
    A group who explores a topic in depth with key stakeholders to gather information directly from them and their common understanding is on various issues.

  • Case Studies
    This is utilized to depict experiences or processes with a view to developing understanding through examination and complex comparison.

Differences


  • Factual - information that is proven to be facts.
  • Analytical - involves logical reasoning of analysis of the information.
  • Subjective - influenced on personal feelings, beliefs or opinions.
  • Objective - contrary to subjectivity, this does not represent one’s own, but what the truth is.



Avoiding Disinformation and Misinformation


Difference of Disinformation and Misinformation

Disinformation and Misinformation are both ways to share false information, the only difference between the two are the reasons behind the spread. The former refers to the common causes of conflict among people with the purpose of harm, containing maliciousness and may spread suspicion or fear with the citizens. However, misinformation can simply mean the data is unsubstantial to be factual but does not have any ill intent to others. It can happen through a misunderstanding or a misinterpretation of a context/subject


Steps to identify misinformation

  1. Examine whether the supporting claims are credible or not
  2. Look at the full context of the situation
  3. Check the sources
  4. Research the sites of the supporting sources
